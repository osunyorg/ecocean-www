Promote your ecological restoration efforts through communication tools and awareness-raising actions.

body: >-


At Ecocean, we restore aquatic environments, but our work doesn't stop there: we also want to raise awareness of this biodiversity and help as many people as possible understand it, by engaging the public through participatory and educational activities.    

Communication tools

Our communication tools help highlight the environmental initiatives we’ve implemented and raise awareness of aquatic biodiversity issues among all audiences. Fully customizable , they can be tailored to each project and serve as effective educational resources to inform, raise awareness, and foster local ownership of environmental initiatives.  

Information stand and Biohut® demonstration module at the port of  Palavas-les-Flots

Information panel in front of the floating ecosystem at the Port of Brussels

A simple wall sign at the port of Marseillan

Floor panel in front of th Biohut® modules at the port of Théoule-sur-Mer

Wooden pedestal panel at the port of Palavas-les-Flots

Awareness days

Our educational events are designed to raise awareness among all audiences about the conservation and restoration of biodiversity. They offer participants the opportunity to explore natural habitats , understand the life cycles of species and the impact of human activities on biodiversity , and take concrete action by participating in Biohut® monitoring or planting on floating ecosystems. 

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=os6L38_jNYY
Ecocean raises children's awareness of marine biodiversity in the port of Chichoulet

Our awareness-raising initiatives carry the 'La Mer en Commun' label.

https://www.mer.gouv.fr/mer-en-commun/la-mer-en-commun

Environmental orientation course

Our environmental education trail offers a chance to explore the local area and its ecosystems, learn while having fun, and gain an understanding of the challenges facing biodiversity and environmental protection through an interactive and hands-on experience .
