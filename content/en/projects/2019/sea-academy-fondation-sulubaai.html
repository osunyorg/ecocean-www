A project to transfer expertise on the BioRestore restocking process, launched around Pangatalan Island in the Philippines, to provide supplementary income for small-scale fishermen in Shark Fin Bay and to support the establishment of marine protected areas in the region.

body: >-

Partners and funders


In response to declining reef populations and increasing pressure on fishery resources, the BioRestore project in Sulubaai aims to restore fish stocks while supporting the livelihoods of local communities.


« Since 2021, Ecocean has been our trusted partner, sharing a common vision of actively restoring marine biodiversity. This long-term support has enabled us to establish our hatchery, train our local team, and implement the method for the first time in this region. »
Laure Thierry de Ville d'Avray
Project manager, Sulubaaï Foundation

The project in pictures

Ecocean
2019 Project launch

- Capture permits - Shipment of equipment - Construction of the breeding farm on Pangatalan Island - Start of the knowledge transfer (fishing, sorting, identification, breeding)

Sulubaaï Fondation
2020-2022 Development - The project continued despite the slowdown caused by COVID - Consolidation of technical protocols - Gradual training of local teams

Ecocean
2023-2024 Launch of the farm - Installation of an operational fish farm (~8 m³) - Comprehensive training of two local technicians - Large-scale deployment of CARE®  

Ecocean
2025 Restocking and ecological monitoring - First releases of pre-grown juveniles - Restocking of new marine protected areas - Launch of scientific ecological monitoring  

2026 Team training - Production of a varied, locally sourced diet (mysis, plankton, fish meal) - Sorting of pre-grown fish into large tanks - Setting up transition habitats
